Where do you need to send the notice to informant?

The informant will use the notice to informant to register the death. The person acting as the informant must be qualified and legally responsible.
People qualified and legally responsible to act as informants

These people are qualified to give information concerning a death under the Births and Deaths Registration Act 1953:

Deaths in houses and public institutions

  • A relative of the deceased, present at the death.
  • A relative of the deceased, in attendance during the last illness.
  • A relative of the deceased, residing or being in the sub-district where the death occurred.
  • A person present at the death.
  • The occupier if they knew of the happening of the death.
  • Any inmate if they knew of the happening of the death.
  • The person arranging the disposal of the body

Deaths in houses and public institutions

  • Any relative of the deceased having knowledge of any of the information required at registration
  • Any person present at the death
  • Any person who found the body
  • Any person in charge of the body
  • The person arranging the disposal of the body.

In relation to a public institution, the occupier includes the:

  • governor
  • keeper
  • master
  • matron
  • superintendent
  • chief resident office
